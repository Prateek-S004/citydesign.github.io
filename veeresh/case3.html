Business Case 3: Healthcare and Well-being

Objectives

  • Allocate healthcare resources to regions with the highest patient counts.
  • Ensure all regions are connected for emergency services.
  • Minimize delays in resource distribution and response times.

Technique: Priority Queue Allocation

This technique uses a max-heap (priority queue) to prioritize resource allocation to regions with the highest patient count first.

Advantages:
  • Time Complexity: O(log n) for each insertion or extraction, where n is the number of regions.
  • Ensures critical regions are served first, reducing delays.
  • Efficient for large datasets and dynamic priority adjustments.

Technique: BFS for Connectivity

BFS explores the city map to ensure all connected regions are covered for emergency service deployment.

Advantages:
  • Time Complexity: O(V + E), where V is the number of regions and E is the number of connections.
  • Fast and reliable for checking connectivity and coverage.
  • Ideal for ensuring efficient routing and resource deployment.
