Business Case 2: Knowledge Sharing Hub

Objectives

  • Effectively assign trainers to centers with the highest demand for skills.
  • Schedule training sessions efficiently to avoid conflicts.
  • Maximize utilization of training center capacity.

Technique: FCFS Scheduling

First-Come-First-Served scheduling ensures training requests are processed in their order of arrival. This minimizes conflicts and improves organization.

Advantages:
  • Time Complexity: O(n), where n is the number of training requests.
  • Simple to implement and fast for small to medium datasets.
  • Provides a fair and predictable schedule.

Efficiency Analysis

The FCFS Scheduling implementation:

  • Space Complexity: O(n), for storing the schedule.
  • Performance: Handles training requests sequentially, ensuring no overlaps or skipped requests.
  • Limitations: Inefficient for high-demand scenarios where dynamic scheduling might be necessary.

Technique: Greedy Resource Allocation

The greedy approach allocates trainers to the training centers with the highest demand for specific skills, ensuring optimal resource utilization.

Advantages:
  • Time Complexity: O(n log n) (sorting by demand).
  • Minimizes trainer idle time and maximizes skill coverage.
  • Works well in balancing resource distribution.

Efficiency Analysis

The Greedy Resource Allocation implementation:

  • Space Complexity: O(n), required for sorting and temporary storage.
  • Performance: Efficient for scenarios with clear prioritization criteria, such as skill demand.
  • Limitations: May fail to account for overlapping constraints or complex dependencies between training centers.
