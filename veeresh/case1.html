Business Case 3: Livable Area Focused on Families

Objectives

  • Optimize accessibility to parks and schools for families.
  • Ensure residential zones are connected efficiently to nearby facilities.
  • Prioritize resource allocation based on population density.

Technique: BFS for Graph Traversal

This technique helps traverse the adjacency matrix of residential zones, parks, and schools. It finds the shortest path between different zones and facilities to optimize accessibility.

Advantages:
  • Time Complexity: O(V + E), where V = number of zones and E = connections.
  • Ensures efficient navigation and traversal.
  • Works well for unweighted graphs and minimizes computation time.

Technique: Greedy Optimization for Resource Allocation

The greedy technique focuses on allocating resources like parks and schools to zones with higher population densities, ensuring maximum benefit.

Advantages:
  • Time Complexity: O(n log n) (sorting by population density).
  • Efficiently maximizes resource impact.
  • Simple and scalable for larger datasets.
